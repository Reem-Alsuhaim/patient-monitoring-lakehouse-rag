Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition in which the force exerted by blood against artery walls remains elevated over time. Persistent hypertension increases the risk of cardiovascular disease, stroke, kidney damage, and heart failure.

Blood pressure is measured using systolic and diastolic values. Elevated readings over an extended period may indicate hypertension. Risk factors include obesity, smoking, excessive salt intake, physical inactivity, diabetes, chronic stress, and family history.

Many individuals with hypertension have no noticeable symptoms, which is why the condition is often referred to as a silent disease. Regular monitoring is essential because complications may develop before symptoms become apparent.

Healthcare professionals manage hypertension through lifestyle modifications and medications. Recommendations often include reducing sodium intake, maintaining a healthy weight, engaging in regular exercise, limiting alcohol consumption, and adhering to prescribed treatment plans.

Long-term monitoring and adherence to therapy are important for reducing complications and improving cardiovascular health outcomes.
